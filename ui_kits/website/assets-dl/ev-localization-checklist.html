Indonesia ties its import-duty and luxury-tax breaks to a local-production commitment, backed by a bank guarantee the carmaker forfeits if it does not deliver. BYD has started trial production at its Subang plant this year; Chery opens its largest ASEAN factory in Vietnam in mid-2026. Thailand and Vietnam are pulling assembly onshore with their own subsidies and duty waivers.

Five questions before you commit

  1. Who on the ground can make a binding decision without calling Europe first?
  2. Have we qualified real suppliers, or only mapped them on a slide?
  3. What is the true lead time to the threshold, counted backward from the deadline?
  4. When something breaks at 2am local time, who fixes it, and do they already know the system?
  5. Who owns the local-content number by name, and what happens if it slips?

If those answers are not clear, you do not have an execution plan yet. You have a strategy, and a clock running against it.
